\begin{abstract}
We propose \emph{Observation Theory}: geometry, distortion, and reliability are
properties of the \emph{observation}---the consumer's read operator, budget, and
channel---not of the object observed. We derive, from a second-order expansion of
the downstream metric, that the operative distortion of a lossy code is the
reconstruction error read through the consumer, $\dO=\tr(\PC\Sd)$, where
$\Sd$ is the error covariance and $\PC=\E[J^\top G\,J]$ the Fisher-metric-weighted
sensitivity of the consumer. Reconstruction distortion is exactly the corner
$\PC=I$; there Shannon rate--distortion and Gauss least-squares live. We work the
theory out from first principles: the read operator is derived for softmax
attention (query second moment on the across-token--demeaned error), for a
gradient-descent optimizer (the loss Hessian, \emph{exactly}), and for retrieval;
the read operator is shown recoverable from the consumer alone by a Jacobian
second moment; the retrieval capacity threshold is derived from extreme-value
theory as $\mu_{\mathrm{crit}}\!\sim\!\sqrt{2\ln N}$; and a consumer-relative
Gaussian rate--distortion function is obtained by reverse water-filling on the
$\PC$-weighted source spectrum, reducing to Shannon at $\PC=I$. This yields a more
general form of the source-coding bound: a consumer-lossless floor
$R_{\mathcal{C}}(0)=H(\Pi X)\le H(X)$, so one can be \emph{lossless to the consumer}
while compressing below the source entropy---the entropy of the read subspace, not
the source, being the true floor; Shannon is the full-read corner
$\ker\PC=\{0\}$, and the information bottleneck the non-geometric generalization.
Each derived face is then tested against a sealed, before-run prediction: distortion
(\texttt{demonstrated}, \texttt{replicated}, out-of-sample), identifiability
($16\times$ chance, blind), capacity (death located to $6\%$), and rate (a
fixed-budget verdict \emph{inverts} under budget-matched observation on real
embedding manifolds). A fifth conjecture---a density quotient restoring the
invariant in non-spectral retrieval---is \emph{refuted} across four prospective
tests and carried as an honest negative. Classical rate--distortion is not wrong;
it is the picture one sees after blurring out who is reading.
\end{abstract}

\section{Introduction}

Shannon fixed a distortion measure---mean-squared error, the same in every
direction---and asked for the fewest bits that meet it \cite{shannon1948,shannon1959}.
Gauss, earlier, fixed the same measure and asked for the estimate that minimizes
the sum of squared residuals \cite{gauss1809}. This paper argues that fixing the
measure is the error. The distortion that matters is not the error a code makes but
the error a consumer \emph{reads}, and different consumers read different subspaces
of the same error. Once the distortion is the one the downstream task induces,
reconstruction is a single corner of a larger theory---the corner where the
consumer is assumed to weight all coordinates equally.

We call this frame \emph{Observation Theory} and develop it in two registers. The
first is analytic: from a second-order expansion (\S\ref{sec:framework}) we obtain a
single controlling quantity, $\tr(\PC\Sd)$, and derive its instances---the read
operator for softmax attention and for a gradient optimizer
(\S\ref{sec:readop}), its recoverability from the consumer alone
(\S\ref{sec:id}), the capacity threshold of single-stage observation
(\S\ref{sec:capacity}), and a consumer-relative rate--distortion function
(\S\ref{sec:rd}). The second register is empirical and adversarial to ourselves:
each derived claim was written down and cryptographically sealed \emph{before} the
measurement that tests it, bars fixed ex ante, misses recorded as misses. Four
faces hold; a fifth is refuted (\S\ref{sec:boundary}); eleven negatives stand with
the same prominence as the positives (\S\ref{sec:negatives}). The recurring
demonstration is a single lossy code whose \emph{downstream verdict inverts} when
nothing changes but the consumer.

\section{The controlling quantity}\label{sec:framework}

\begin{definition}[Instance]
An instance is $(X,\mathcal{C},\mathcal{Q})$: a representation $X$ with vectors
$x\in\R^D$, a \emph{consumer} $\mathcal{C}:\R^D\to\R^m$ with a divergence $D_Y$ on
its outputs, and a family $\mathcal{Q}$ of lossy codes producing $\hat x=x+\delta$.
The downstream distortion is $\dO:=D_Y(\mathcal{C}(x),\mathcal{C}(\hat x))$,
measured on the consumer output, never assumed from the ambient metric of $X$.
\end{definition}

\begin{proposition}[Consumer-projected distortion]\label{prop:main}
Let $D_Y$ be twice differentiable with $D_Y(u,u)=0$ and $\nabla_v D_Y(u,v)|_{v=u}=0$,
and let $G(u)=\nabla^2_v D_Y(u,v)|_{v=u}\succeq0$ be its output information metric.
Let $J(x)=\partial\mathcal{C}/\partial x$. For a code with error $\delta=\hat x-x$
whose conditional covariance given $x$ is $\Sd(x)$,
\[
\dO \;=\; \tfrac12\,\tr\!\big(\PC\,\Sd\big)\;+\;o(\|\delta\|^2),
\qquad
\PC \;:=\; \E_x\!\big[\,J(x)^\top G(\mathcal{C}(x))\,J(x)\,\big],
\quad
\Sd=\E_x[\Sd(x)],
\]
provided $\Sd(x)$ is (conditionally) uncorrelated with the point-dependent weight.
Reconstruction distortion $\E\|\delta\|^2=\tr(\Sd)$ is the case $\PC=I$
($G=I$, $J=I$).
\end{proposition}

\begin{proof}
Expand $\mathcal{C}(x+\delta)=\mathcal{C}(x)+J(x)\delta+O(\|\delta\|^2)$ and
$D_Y(\mathcal{C}(x),\cdot)$ to second order about its minimum. The value and
gradient terms vanish by $D_Y(u,u)=0$, $\nabla_vD_Y|_{v=u}=0$, leaving
$D_Y(\mathcal{C}(x),\mathcal{C}(x+\delta))=\tfrac12(J\delta)^\top G(J\delta)
+o(\|\delta\|^2)=\tfrac12\,\delta^\top\!\big(J^\top G J\big)\delta+o(\|\delta\|^2)$.
Take $\E_x$; under the stated uncorrelatedness $\E_x[\delta^\top(J^\top GJ)\delta]
=\tr\!\big(\E_x[J^\top GJ]\,\E_x[\Sd(x)]\big)=\tr(\PC\Sd)$.
\end{proof}

Proposition~\ref{prop:main} is the theory in one line: \emph{the distortion that
governs a task is the reconstruction-error covariance projected onto the consumer's
Fisher-weighted sensitivity $\PC$.} The rest of the paper derives $\PC$ for
concrete consumers, shows it is recoverable, and studies what the observer's
channel and budget do to it. (We drop the harmless factor $\tfrac12$.)

\section{The read operator, derived}\label{sec:readop}

\begin{proposition}[Softmax attention]\label{prop:softmax}
Let a consumer read keys $\{k_s\}_{s=1}^{S}$ through a query bank: logits
$l_s=q^\top k_s/\sqrt{D}$, output $p=\mathrm{softmax}(l)$, divergence
$D_Y=\mathrm{KL}$. Under a key perturbation $\delta_s$,
\[
\dO \;=\; \tfrac{1}{2D}\,\E_q\,\mathrm{Var}^{\,p}_s\!\big(q^\top\delta_s\big)
\;+\;O(\|\delta\|^3)
\;=\;\tfrac{1}{2D}\,\tr\!\big(Q\,\bar\Sigma_\delta\big)+O(\|\delta\|^3),
\]
where $Q=\E_q[qq^\top]$ is the query second moment and
$\bar\Sigma_\delta=\sum_s p_s\,\tilde\delta_s\tilde\delta_s^\top$ is the
$p$-weighted covariance of the \emph{across-token--demeaned} errors
$\tilde\delta_s=\delta_s-\sum_{s'}p_{s'}\delta_{s'}$.
\end{proposition}
\begin{proof}
The Fisher metric of $\mathrm{softmax}$ is $G=\mathrm{diag}(p)-pp^\top$, so
$\mathrm{KL}(p\|\mathrm{softmax}(l+\Delta l))=\tfrac12\Delta l^\top(\mathrm{diag}(p)
-pp^\top)\Delta l+O(\Delta l^3)=\tfrac12\mathrm{Var}^p_s(\Delta l_s)$ with
$\Delta l_s=q^\top\delta_s/\sqrt D$. The $-pp^\top$ term is exactly the softmax's
invariance to a common additive logit shift; it removes the $p$-mean of $\delta_s$,
yielding the demeaned $\tilde\delta_s$. Averaging $q^\top\tilde\delta_s
\tilde\delta_s^\top q$ over $q$ gives $\tr(Q\,\bar\Sigma_\delta)$.
\end{proof}
Two consequences are exactly the phenomena we later measure. First, $\PC\propto Q$:
an \emph{isotropic} query bank gives $\PC\propto I$ (reconstruction-like), while a
subspace-concentrated bank projects onto that subspace---so the read operator, and
hence the winning code, changes with the query distribution. Second, only the
\emph{demeaned} (tangential) part of the error survives; the across-token mean is a
nuisance the softmax cannot see.

\begin{proposition}[Gradient-descent optimizer; exact]\label{prop:opt}
Let $L(\theta)=\tfrac12\theta^\top H\theta$ with $H\succeq0$, gradient $g=H\theta$,
and a compressed gradient $\hat g=g+\delta$ used in one step of size $\eta$. Then
\[
L(\theta-\eta\hat g)-L(\theta-\eta g)
=\tfrac12\eta^2\,\delta^\top H\delta-\eta\,\delta^\top H(\theta-\eta g),
\]
whose error-quadratic part is $\tfrac12\eta^2\,\tr(H\,\delta\delta^\top)$; averaging
the linear cross term over zero-mean, direction-uncorrelated error gives
$\E[\dO]=\tfrac12\eta^2\,\tr(H\,\Sd)$, so $\PC=H$ \emph{exactly}.
\end{proposition}
\begin{proof}
With $\theta^+=\theta-\eta g$, expand $L(\theta^+-\eta\delta)=L(\theta^+)-\eta
\delta^\top H\theta^+ +\tfrac12\eta^2\delta^\top H\delta$; the cross term is linear
in $\delta$ and vanishes in expectation, leaving $\tfrac12\eta^2\,\tr(H\,\Sd)$ with
no higher-order remainder.
\end{proof}
Here $\PC=H$ \emph{exactly}: the loss curvature \emph{is} the read operator, with no
second-order remainder. Reconstruction ($\tr\Sd$) is the flat-loss case $H=I$; when
the loss is curved, a gradient code good in flat directions and bad in sharp ones is
downstream-worse than its reconstruction admits.

\begin{remark}[Retrieval]
For score-ranking consumers $s(q,x_i)=q^\top x_i$, the fraction of inverted pairs is,
to leading order for smooth rank statistics, monotone in
$\tr(\PC\bar\Sigma_\delta)/\tr(\PC\bar\Sigma_0)$ with $\PC=\E[qq^\top]$ and
$\bar\Sigma$ the across-item covariance---the same read operator, now a subspace
projector when the query distribution is concentrated. (Rank statistics are
non-smooth; we treat this instance empirically, \S\ref{sec:distortion}.)
\end{remark}

\section{Distortion: the consumer is the metric (GO-2)}\label{sec:distortion}

Proposition~\ref{prop:softmax} predicts that at \emph{matched reconstruction} the
downstream ranking of two codes is set by $\tr(\PC\Sd)$, hence by the query
distribution. We test it under sealed registration.

\paragraph{Dissociation.} Two 4-bit codes at an audited matched budget (spread
$0.19$ bit) with \emph{identical} reconstruction ($0.0938$ vs $0.0934$): the
whole-vector code is $2.53\times$ worse on softmax-KL ($12/12$ seeds); a
wrong-quotient control is $21\times$ worse at $\sim\!5\times$ the reconstruction.
Reconstruction is not the controlling quantity (\texttt{[demonstrated]}).

\paragraph{Flip.} Holding reconstruction fixed and changing only the query bank,
the ranking \emph{inverts}: isotropic $\Rightarrow$ code $a$ better ($2.5\times$,
$12/12$); subspace $\Rightarrow$ code $b$ better ($12/12$). The directly computed
$\tr(\PC\Sd)$ tracks the sign on every seed in both consumers; reconstruction, a
consumer-blind scalar, cannot (rank correlation $0.40$ vs $1.00$). This is
Proposition~\ref{prop:softmax} with $Q$ swapped, observed
(\texttt{[demonstrated]}).

\paragraph{Replication and out-of-sample.} The flip replicates on retrieval ranking
(different representation, set-valued consumer): identical reconstruction
($0.0964$), ranking flips with the read subspace (log-advantage $-4.70$ vs $+4.65$,
$12/12$), $\tr(\PC\Sd)$ tracking, reconstruction blind (\texttt{[replicated]}). It
generalizes to optimization with $\PC=H$ (Proposition~\ref{prop:opt}): at matched
reconstruction the curvature-metric distortion flips between two Hessians ($12/12$),
tracked by $\tr(H\Sd)$, reconstruction uninformative ($-0.20$)---and under $H=I$
reconstruction tracks \emph{perfectly} ($1.00$), the derived $\PC=I$ corner,
observed (\texttt{[predicted]}, out-of-sample). One derivation; three read operators.

\section{Identifiability: the metric is recoverable (GO-1)}\label{sec:id}

Proposition~\ref{prop:main} is useful only if $\PC$ is knowable before compressing.

\begin{proposition}[Recovery of the read subspace]\label{prop:id}
Let $\mathcal{C}(x)=f(Wx)$ with $W\in\R^{r\times D}$ of rank $r$ and $f$
coordinatewise. Then $J(x)^\top J(x)=W^\top\!\operatorname{diag}(f'(Wx)^2)\,W$ and
$\E_x[J^\top J]=W^\top\!\E[\operatorname{diag}(f'^2)]\,W$ has rank $r$ with column
space equal to $\operatorname{row}(W)$. Hence the top-$r$ eigenvectors of
$\E_x[J^\top J]$ span the read subspace, and a finite-difference estimate
$\hat J u\approx(\mathcal{C}(x{+}hu)-\mathcal{C}(x{-}hu))/2h$ recovers it from
consumer queries alone.
\end{proposition}

We built such a probe---receiving only the callable $\mathcal{C}(\cdot)$ and the
dimensions, never $W$, the codes, or $\dO$ (blinding enforced in code). It recovers
the planted read subspace at overlap $0.936$ against a $0.059$ chance baseline
($16\times$), and the \emph{blind} $\tr(\hat\PC\Sd)$ predicts the flip of
\S\ref{sec:distortion} on $12/12$ seeds in both consumers with rank correlation
$1.00$---identical to ground truth---while reconstruction cannot ($0.40$)
(\texttt{[predicted]}). Composed: from the consumer alone one predicts which code
wins, before compressing anything.

\section{Capacity: a vacuity threshold, derived (GO-3)}\label{sec:capacity}

Observation has a reliability limit, and it too is computable in advance.

\begin{proposition}[Retrieval vacuity threshold]\label{prop:capacity}
For single-stage top-1 retrieval with $N\!-\!1$ conditionally-exchangeable
distractor scores standardized to mean $0$, variance $1$, success requires the
standardized true margin to exceed $M_{N-1}=\max$ of the distractors, and
\[
\mu_{\mathrm{crit}}:=\E[M_{N-1}]
=\sqrt{2\ln(N{-}1)}-\frac{\ln\ln(N{-}1)+\ln4\pi}{2\sqrt{2\ln(N{-}1)}}+o\!\Big(\tfrac{1}{\sqrt{\ln N}}\Big).
\]
Writing the margin certificate $\hat\mu=(\overline{\text{true}}-\overline{\text{distr}})/\mathrm{std}$
and $\rho=\hat\mu/\mu_{\mathrm{crit}}$, recall crosses $\tfrac12$ at $\rho\!\approx\!1$;
the certificate is \emph{vacuous} for $\rho<1$.
\end{proposition}
\begin{proof}[Derivation]
Standardized i.i.d.\ Gaussian maxima obey the Fisher--Tippett law with Gumbel
limit and centering/scaling $a_N=\sqrt{2\ln(N{-}1)}$,
$b_N=1/a_N$; $\E[M_{N-1}]=a_N-b_N(\ln\ln(N{-}1)+\ln4\pi)/2+o(b_N)$. Top-1 success is
$\text{true}>M_{N-1}$; averaging the standardized margin gives $\hat\mu$, so the
$0.5$-recall crossing is at $\hat\mu\approx\E[M_{N-1}]=\mu_{\mathrm{crit}}$.
\end{proof}

Across $14$ structurally distinct corpora the derived---not fitted---threshold locates
death: the recall-$0.5$ crossing sits at $\rho=0.948$ (within $6\%$ of $1$); the
certificate orders corpora by recall at rank correlation $0.991$, beating the
un-normalized margin ($0.873$), with a finite-width transition
(\texttt{[demonstrated]}). Capacity says, from a computable quantity, \emph{where}
the observation channel dies before any retrieval is run.

\section{Rate: the verdict belongs to the budget (GO-4)}\label{sec:rate}

The read operator, capacity, and distortion are stated at a fixed observer budget;
the budget is itself a rate, and it can invert the verdict. The mechanism is
spectral. An eigenfunction $\varphi_k$ of a Laplace--Beltrami operator with
eigenvalue $\lambda_k$ oscillates on the \emph{wavelength} $\lambda_k^{-1/2}$;
Weyl's law $N(\lambda)\sim c\,\mathrm{Vol}(M)\,\lambda^{d/2}$ says the count of modes
below a fixed scale grows with resolution. Geodesic distance is preserved by modes
whose wavelength \emph{exceeds} the geodesic scale of interest (small $\lambda$);
modes with $\lambda$ above a threshold $\tau$ oscillate \emph{below} that scale and
encode sub-geodesic detail.

\begin{lemma}[Budget inversion]\label{lem:rate}
Fix a low-mode budget $m$ (a fixed low-$\lambda$ band). As the sample $N$ grows, the
graph operator converges to the manifold operator and the fixed band \emph{sharpens},
so geodesic fidelity is nondecreasing. Hold instead the resolved scale $\tau$ fixed
(spectral-gap matching), so $m=N(\tau)$ grows with $N$: the newly resolved modes
near $\tau$ have wavelength approaching the sampling scale and, for a non-ideal
(finite-sample, noisy) substrate, inject sub-geodesic detail, so geodesic fidelity
is nonincreasing. The two budgets coincide at the coarsest $N$ and diverge in
\emph{opposite} directions thereafter.
\end{lemma}

We test Lemma~\ref{lem:rate} on real $768$-d book-paragraph embedding manifolds
($32{,}000$ points, three seeds). Fixed budget $m=10$: geometry preservation rises
with $N$ (trend $+0.4,+1.0,+1.0$). Spectral-gap-matched, $m:10\to121/126/159$:
geometry \emph{collapses} (trend $-1.0$ every seed), the added high modes injecting
sub-wavelength detail; the budgets agree at the smallest $N$ (gap $\le0.002$)
(\texttt{[replicated]}). Opposite verdicts about the same data, set by the observer's
rate: ``clean geometry'' is a relation between observer resolution and substrate
scale, not a substrate invariant.

\section{A consumer-relative rate--distortion function}\label{sec:rd}

The four faces assemble into one object. For a Gaussian source and the
consumer-projected distortion of Proposition~\ref{prop:main}:

\begin{proposition}[Consumer-relative Gaussian rate--distortion]\label{prop:rd}
Let $x\sim\mathcal{N}(0,\Sigma_x)$ and constrain $\tr(\PC\Sd)\le D$. In the basis
diagonalizing $\PC^{1/2}\Sigma_x\PC^{1/2}$ with eigenvalues $\gamma_i$, the minimum
rate is
\[
R_{\mathcal{C}}(D)=\sum_i \tfrac12\log_2^{+}\!\frac{\gamma_i}{\theta},\qquad
\sum_i\min(\gamma_i,\theta)=D,
\]
a \emph{reverse water-filling on the $\PC$-weighted source spectrum}. For $\PC=I$
this is Shannon's Gaussian rate--distortion function \cite{shannon1959}, and the
estimation dual is Gauss--Markov least squares \cite{gauss1809}.
\end{proposition}
\begin{proof}[Derivation]
$\tr(\PC\Sd)=\tr(\PC^{1/2}\Sd\PC^{1/2})$; change variables $y=\PC^{1/2}x$ so the
distortion is ordinary MSE on $y$ with source covariance $\PC^{1/2}\Sigma_x\PC^{1/2}$.
Shannon's Gaussian water-filling applies to $y$, giving the stated allocation; the
transform is invertible off the null space of $\PC$, on which the consumer is blind
and no rate is spent.
\end{proof}

Proposition~\ref{prop:rd} makes the placement exact: \emph{classical
rate--distortion is Observation Theory at $\PC=I$}. Bits should water-fill on the
directions the consumer reads \emph{and} the source varies ($\gamma_i$ large), and
spend nothing on $\ker\PC$---which is precisely why, empirically, a
reconstruction-optimal code ($\PC=I$ allocation) is beaten by a consumer-matched one
whenever $\PC\ne I$, and why the winner \emph{flips} with $\PC$
(\S\ref{sec:distortion}). The identity-curvature control of \S\ref{sec:distortion}
is the clean confirmation: only at $\PC=I$ does reconstruction become the correct
objective, and there it becomes it perfectly.

\subsection*{A more general Shannon: lossless to the consumer below the source entropy}

Proposition~\ref{prop:rd} departs from Shannon in a way worth stating sharply.
Shannon source coding obeys $R(0)=H(X)$: lossless representation costs the full
source entropy. Observation Theory does not.

\begin{proposition}[Consumer-lossless floor]\label{prop:floor}
Let $\Pi$ project orthogonally onto $\operatorname{range}(\PC)$, rank $r\le D$.
There exist codes with $\tr(\PC\Sd)=0$---zero downstream distortion---at rate
\[
R_{\mathcal{C}}(0)=H(\Pi X)\;\le\;H(X),
\qquad
H(X)-R_{\mathcal{C}}(0)=H\!\big(X\mid \Pi X\big),
\]
with equality iff $\PC\succ0$. The saving is the entropy of the consumer-invisible
subspace $\ker\PC$, discarded at no downstream cost.
\end{proposition}
\begin{proof}
$\tr(\PC\Sd)=\tr(\PC\,\Pi\Sd\Pi)$ depends on $\delta$ only through $\Pi\delta$; a
code that transmits $\Pi X$ losslessly and reconstructs $(\mathrm{I}-\Pi)X$ by its
conditional mean has $\Pi\delta=0$, hence $\tr(\PC\Sd)=0$, at rate $H(\Pi X)$.
\end{proof}

This is a genuinely more general statement than Shannon's: \emph{one can be lossless
to the consumer while compressing below the source entropy}, and the floor is the
entropy of the \emph{read subspace}, not of the source. It is what the flip
experiments exhibit---a code spending zero bits on $\ker\PC$ (the across-token mean
the softmax cannot see, Prop.~\ref{prop:softmax}; the subspace the query bank never
probes) loses nothing the consumer can detect, and beats a reconstruction-optimal
code that wastes bits there. Shannon is the corner $\ker\PC=\{0\}$, where nothing is
free.

Placed against the literature, Observation Theory is the middle term of a chain:
Shannon rate--distortion ($\PC=I$) $\subset$ consumer-relative rate--distortion
($\PC$ a rank-$r$ \emph{geometric, identifiable} operator, Props.~\ref{prop:rd},
\ref{prop:floor}) $\subset$ the information bottleneck \cite{tishby1999} (an
arbitrary relevance variable $Y$, generally non-geometric and requiring the joint
$p(X,Y)$). The middle term is the operational one: $\PC$ is the \emph{local Fisher
metric} of the relevance $\mathcal{C}(X)$ (Prop.~\ref{prop:main}), it is PSD and
water-fillable (Prop.~\ref{prop:rd}), and---unlike the abstract bottleneck---it is
\emph{recoverable from the consumer alone} by probing (Prop.~\ref{prop:id}). A more
general Shannon is thus not a slogan but a program: replace ``represent the source''
with ``serve the reader,'' make the reader's metric a measurable geometric object,
and recover source coding, the reliability limit of \S\ref{sec:capacity} (a
consumer-relative channel bound), and least squares as the $\PC=I$ shadow. What
remains for the converse is a coding theorem: that $R_{\mathcal{C}}(D)$ is
\emph{achievable} for general (non-Gaussian) sources by a random-coding argument on
the $\PC$-tilted typical set---the natural next theorem of this program.

\section{A boundary: the refuted quotient (GO-5)}\label{sec:boundary}

Not every plausible face survives a sealed prediction. In the spectral setting the
$\alpha{=}1$ anisotropic normalization of a diffusion operator cancels sampling
density and recovers density-free geometry \cite{coifman2006}; the null space of
$\PC$ there is the density mode. We conjectured this \emph{density quotient} likewise
restores the invariant in a \emph{non-spectral} domain (locally-scaled ADC
retrieval). It does not. Across four prospective tests---direct affinity (two
variants), product-quantization ADC on real embeddings, and non-spectral
diffusion-distance retrieval---the quotient fails to produce a decisive,
density-specific restoration. In the direct and ADC domains it \emph{hurts}: the
retrieval base is not a density-biased operator, so there is no density term to
cancel, and in concentrated real data density is entangled with the invariant, so
down-weighting dense points removes signal. In the diffusion domain the direction is
right---$\alpha{=}1$ is the optimal exponent, recovered non-spectrally by iterated
transition-matrix powers---but the effect is under $2\%$ and \emph{not
density-specific} (a uniform-density control gains as much, $+0.015$ vs $+0.019$).
Per its sealed registration the conjecture is \texttt{[refuted]} (NEG-11). The
sharpened statement: \emph{the $\alpha{=}1$ cancellation is a property of the
diffusion operator and does not transfer to direct observation}---consistent with
Proposition~\ref{prop:rd}, which spends zero rate on $\ker\PC$ only when $\PC$
actually contains the density direction. The theory is stronger for the boundary.

\section{Honest negatives}\label{sec:negatives}

Eleven standing negatives bound the theory and are reported with the prominence of
the positives. Reconstruction cosine is not a proxy for key quality (the motivating
negative). The reconstruction-blind flip is observable only for
reconstruction-\emph{matched} codes: absent a tie one code Pareto-dominates and
reconstruction is not falsified (NEG-10, a prospective miss locating a precondition,
consistent with Proposition~\ref{prop:rd}'s water-filling). The projected-variance
\emph{trace} governs the discriminating comparison but is not a complete rank
statistic over all codes (NEG-9). The density quotient (NEG-11, \S\ref{sec:boundary}).
Two are methodological: twice a substantive claim passed at $12/12$ while an
over-strict secondary gate we had written failed, and twice a degenerate substrate
was caught only by a regime-coverage gate---reported because a theory that publishes
only its wins cannot be believed about them.

\section{Method: registration-first}\label{sec:method}

Every empirical claim was governed by a dated, hashed registry entry
committed---sealed---before the first measurement it governs; the commit is the
binding timestamp. Bars were fixed ex ante; misses enter the ledger as misses;
amendments only dated and logged before unblinding; prospective claims
(\S\ref{sec:id},\ref{sec:rate},\ref{sec:boundary}) were blinded where feasible. The
registry, ledger, harnesses, and result artifacts are public; the house rule is that
the paper may not assert what the ledger cannot show. This is what lets a reader
separate the four faces that held from the fifth that did not.

\section{Discussion}\label{sec:discussion}

Observation Theory asks a change of default. Where a pipeline minimizes
reconstruction and hopes the task follows, the theory says: recover the consumer's
read operator (\S\ref{sec:id}), water-fill bits on $\PC$ rather than on $I$
(\S\ref{sec:rd}), respect the channel's vacuity threshold (\S\ref{sec:capacity}), and
remember the verdict is relative to the observation budget (\S\ref{sec:rate}). The
practical target is compression, quantization, and retrieval, where reconstruction
reigns and is, we have shown analytically and empirically, the wrong objective when
$\PC\ne I$. The theoretical content is a reframing: geometry, distortion, and
reliability are relations between an object and an observer, and the observer is
specifiable, recoverable, and---within its budget and channel---sovereign over the
verdict. Open: the achievability and converse of $R_{\mathcal{C}}(D)$ beyond the
Gaussian case; whether the flip is exactly its argmin migrating with $\PC$; and the
hardening of \S\ref{sec:id}--\ref{sec:distortion} on trained-model consumers.
Four faces, one refutation, eleven negatives: a world to map, not a slogan.
