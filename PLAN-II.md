## W2 — The decisive validations (T+2 → T+9 mo) — where "next level" lives

- **V1 — The trained-model flip.** GO-P-2026-021 showed worse-arm
  discrimination under one consumer; the theory's signature is the *inversion*.
  Design: one Llama model, two query banks drawn from two real usage
  distributions (distinct task families inducing distinct Q), two
  reconstruction-matched key codes; sealed prediction that the downstream
  ranking inverts with the bank and that blind tr(P̂_C Σ_δ) calls both signs.
  This is the single highest-value experiment in the program.
- **V2 — Gate B, out-of-sample #1: Adam moments.** Consumer = the optimizer
  update as a function of stored (m, v); P_C derivable analytically from the
  update rule and independently probeable — analytic-vs-probe agreement is
  itself a registered sub-claim. Full three-arm design (invariant-preserving /
  reconstruction-optimal / anti-probe) at matched bits; downstream metric =
  training-trajectory divergence and final loss. Seal before any real-data
  pilot.
- **V3 — a named, NON-GATING possibility: the swell radar.** An *idea only* — a
  hypothetical physical-sensing Gate-B domain, not a real system and not on any
  critical path. **The plan does not gate on it, does not schedule it, and does

  adoption bet). Listed so the idea isn't lost; if it ever becomes concrete, its

## W3 — Flagship theorems (parallel; pick TWO, name the rest)

Ranked by (reachability × operational closure):

- **T1 — Learn-then-serve (recommended #1).** Probe sample complexity: matrix
  Bernstein on the finite-difference Jacobian estimator + Davis–Kahan on the
  recovered subspace, composed with the mismatch appendix (commission tax +
  omission floor as functions of principal angles — the floor now
  `[demonstrated]` downstream on a trained model, GO-P-2026-027) into one
  end-to-end statement: *n consumer queries under distribution π buy operating
  point R(D) + ε(n, π) with certified floor bounds.* Caveat inherited from the
  E-limits finding: the downstream floor is a high-resolution phenomenon whose
  finite-rate visibility scales with the omitted direction's weight, so ε(n, π)
  must carry the rate-regime dependence, not principal angles alone. Completes
  §V → Appendix-mismatch into a pipeline theorem; ISIT-sized on its own.
- **T2 — Consumer-relative channel coding (the §VI dual).** Define the
  observation channel and prove the capacity theorem that turns the
  extreme-value vacuity threshold into an operational limit — the missing
  third leg of the Shannon parallel (source ✓, separation ✓, channel ✗).
  Hard; highest prestige; T-IT paper #2 if it lands.
- **T3 — Common observation.** Gray–Wyner with two read operators: the
  common layer two observers share, Wyner common information of the two
  quotients. The simultaneous-broadcast topology to §10's sequential one.
- **T4 — Non-Gaussian region** (log-concave sources via EPI-type tools).
  Named, not chased, per charter scope discipline.

Choose T1 + one of {T2, T3}. Every proof: fresh-context pass + C3 numerical
harness + ledger row before any paper asserts it.
